Beyond the Scheduled Stops

Cocktail Bars

Edinburgh has one of the best cocktail scenes in the UK — these are the ones worth seeking out

🐼

Panda & Sons

World's Best Bar 2025

79 Queen Street, Edinburgh EH2 4NF

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's most iconic speakeasy — hidden behind a bookshelf in what appears to be an old barbershop. Named World's Best Bar in 2025, this is a rite of passage for any serious Edinburgh visitor. Ring the bell, find the bookcase, and prepare to be blown away.

Tip: Ring the doorbell of the "barbershop" and push the bookshelf to enter. Book ahead — it fills up fast.
🌿

Bramble Bar

Underground Speakeasy

16A Queen Street, Edinburgh EH2 1JE

📍 View on map →

The original Edinburgh speakeasy that started it all. Hidden in a New Town basement with tea lights, alcoves, and exposed brick, Bramble has been one of the city's most beloved cocktail bars since 2006. Classic, unpretentious, and absolutely brilliant.

Tip: No sign outside — look for the steps down on Queen Street, just doors from Panda & Sons. Do both in one evening!
🍋

Hey Palu

UK Top 50 · #17

49 Bread Street, Edinburgh EH3 9AH

📍 View on map →

A love letter to Italian aperitivo culture — ranked #17 in the UK Top 50 cocktail bars. Famous for its extraordinary amaro collection, frozen Martinis straight from the freezer, and Italian-inspired nibbles. Chic, intimate, and utterly delicious.

Tip: Try an amaro flight if you've never explored Italian bitters — the bar team are passionate guides.
🧙

The Alchemist

Theatrical Cocktails

St James Quarter & George Street, Edinburgh

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's most theatrical cocktail experience — think dry ice, flames, smoking drinks and novelty props. Every drink is a performance. Not the most nuanced cocktail bar in the city, but wildly fun and extremely Instagrammable.

Tip: The St James Quarter location is steps from Duck & Waffle — perfect for a pre or post-lunch drink on Day 1.
🚪

Never Really Here

No-Menu Speakeasy

Rose Street North Lane, Edinburgh EH2

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's most secretive bar — no menu, no sign, no fanfare. Just an unmarked door on Rose Street North Lane. Tell the bartender what you're feeling and they'll craft something bespoke just for you. One of the most special bar experiences in Scotland.

Tip: Small capacity — arrive early or you may not get in. Strict policy on group sizes.
🎸

Stramash

Free Live Music Nightly

207 Cowgate, Edinburgh EH1 1JQ

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's greatest live music bar — a massive 900-capacity converted church on the Cowgate with live bands playing every single night until 3am, completely free. Two floors, a huge stage, mezzanine bar, and a bouncing dancefloor. Wednesday nights feature a traditional ceilidh. If you want to dance until 3am after the Voodoo Rooms or Department of Magic, this is where you end up.

Tip: Free entry every night — just show up. Wednesday ceilidh is brilliant for a first-timer, even better after a few drams.
🎵

Sneaky Pete's

Edinburgh Institution

73 Cowgate, Edinburgh EH1 1JW

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's most legendary grassroots music club — a tiny 100-capacity basement on the Cowgate where Lewis Capaldi and Mumford & Sons played their early gigs. Named by DJ Magazine as one of the most important clubs in the world. Dark, brick-lined, loud, and absolutely electric. Gigs from 7pm nightly, club nights until 3am.

Tip: Check their website for the weekly lineup — the gig programme is exceptional. Right next door to Stramash for a double hit of live music.
🎭

Encore Speakeasy

Most Glamorous

4 Hope Street, Edinburgh EH2 4DB

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's most glamorous secret bar — a Moulin Rouge-inspired underground speakeasy hidden below the West End Brasserie. Enter through a picture frame door into red velvet sofas, gold trim booths, a "Press for Cocktails" button, and theatrical Parisian-inspired cocktails. Live DJs playing disco, funk, soul and R&B on weekends. Pure opulence.

Tip: Book ahead — fills up fast on weekends. Dress up, sportswear not allowed. The art deco photo booth is a must!

Scotland's Liquid Gold

Whisky Experiences

You're in Scotland — you have to drink whisky. Here's where to do it properly

🏰

Johnnie Walker Princes Street

Must Do

145 Princes Street, Edinburgh EH2 4BL

📍 View on map →

A stunning five-floor whisky experience in the heart of Edinburgh with the best castle views in the city from the rooftop 1820 bar. Take an immersive whisky tour and sample three whisky cocktails, then head to the rooftop terrace for sunset views of Edinburgh Castle. One of the best experiences in the city.

Tip: Book the tour in advance — and anyone can visit the 1820 rooftop bar without a tour. Go at sunset!
🥃

The Scotch Whisky Experience

Royal Mile

354 Castlehill, Royal Mile, Edinburgh EH1 2NE

Right at the top of the Royal Mile next to Edinburgh Castle, this world-class visitor centre takes you on a barrel ride through the history of Scotch whisky. Choose from a range of tasting experiences — from a quick introduction to a deep dive into Scotland's four whisky regions.

Tip: The Silver Tour includes four regional whisky tastings and takes about 75 minutes — perfect for a half-afternoon.
🍾

Royal Mile Whiskies

Shopping

379 High Street, Royal Mile, Edinburgh EH1 1PW

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's finest whisky shop — stocking hundreds of expressions from rare single malts to blended Scotch. Perfect for picking up a bottle to bring home or finding something truly special. The knowledgeable staff will help you find exactly the right dram.

Tip: Look for limited edition releases and exclusive bottlings you won't find at home.

Pit Stops & Pick-Me-Ups

Coffee & Cafés

Edinburgh's café scene is exceptional — these are the ones worth ducking into

🥛

The Milkman

Most Instagrammable

52 Cockburn Street, Edinburgh EH1 1PB

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's most charming café — tucked in a beautifully restored old sweet shop on Cockburn Street with curved windows made famous on Instagram. The coffee is excellent and the morning buns are legendary. A perfect rest stop right near the Royal Mile.

Tip: Grab a window seat to watch the world go by on one of Edinburgh's most photogenic streets. Go early to beat the queues.
🥐

La Barantine

French Patisserie

Old Town, Edinburgh

An authentic French artisan bakery in the heart of the Old Town, run by French pâtissiers. Everything is handmade — croissants, pain au chocolat, Paris-Brest, lemon tarts, and éclairs. The most genuine French café experience outside of Paris, right in Edinburgh's cobbled streets.

Tip: Go in the morning for the freshest pastries — they sell out fast.
🍂

Söderberg

Swedish Café

Multiple locations, Edinburgh

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's beloved Swedish café — utterly beautiful Scandi-inspired interiors with fluffy cardamom buns filled with almond paste and whipped cream alongside silky flat whites. Warm, unhurried, and the perfect spot to rest tired feet between sightseeing.

Tip: The Grassmarket location is the most atmospheric — perfect after a browse of Victoria Street.
🧪

Brew Lab Coffee

Specialty Roaster

6–8 South College Street, Edinburgh EH8 9AA

📍 View on map →

One of Edinburgh's first and finest third-wave coffee shops — a minimalist, lab-like space near the National Museum of Scotland where the baristas treat every cup like a masterclass. Single origin beans, precise brewing methods, and a great food menu of toasties and bagels. Perfect for a serious coffee stop in the Old Town.

Tip: Ask the baristas about their current beans — they genuinely love talking about it. Steps from the National Museum.
🏔️

Cairngorm Coffee

New Town Favourite

1 Melville Place, Edinburgh EH3 7PR

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's brightest and most beautiful café — a gorgeous corner space in the New Town with an entire wall of windows flooding the room with light. Named after Scotland's famous mountain range, they roast their own beans in the Cairngorms using the first San Remo Opera machine in Scotland. Exceptional coffee, healthy food options, and a wonderfully airy vibe.

Tip: The Melville Place location is the most beautiful — a perfect stop on the way to Dean Village. Also has a Frederick Street location in central New Town.

For the Hungry Wanderer

Snacks & Bites

When hunger strikes between activities — Edinburgh has some excellent quick stops

🍦

Mary's Milk Bar

Edinburgh Institution

19 Grassmarket, Edinburgh EH1 2HS

📍 View on map →

Voted the best ice cream in the UK — a tiny retro gelato parlour in the Grassmarket with views straight up to Edinburgh Castle. Mary trained at the Carpigiani Gelato University in Bologna and makes new flavours every morning. Expect queues, but they move fast and every scoop is worth it. Try the spaghetti gelato — a scoop presented as spaghetti with cherry "meatballs" and white chocolate "parmesan."

Tip: Check their Instagram the morning of your visit to see that day's flavours. Open Wed–Sun 11am–7:30pm.
🐟

Bertie's Fish & Chips

Classic Scottish

Victoria Street, Edinburgh

📍 View on map →

Proper Scottish fish and chips — crispy battered haddock, golden chips, and mushy peas on Victoria Street. Sometimes you just need the most perfectly executed version of a classic. This is it. Grab a paper cone and eat them on the Grassmarket steps watching Edinburgh Castle.

Tip: Get the haggis and chips if you want the full Scottish experience.
🧀

I.J. Mellis Cheesemonger

Artisan Food

30A Victoria Street, Edinburgh EH1 2JW

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's legendary artisan cheesemonger on Victoria Street — a cave-like shop bursting with extraordinary farmhouse and artisan cheeses from Scotland and beyond. Pick up a wedge of mature Scottish cheddar or a creamy soft cheese with oatcakes for the perfect impromptu picnic.

Tip: The staff will let you taste before you buy — ask for a recommendation and they'll find your perfect cheese.
🍫

The Chocolatarium

Viral on TikTok

3–5 Cranston Street, Edinburgh EH8 8BE

📍 View on map →

Scotland's most talked-about chocolate experience — a 90-minute immersive tour at a micro-factory just off the Royal Mile. Taste over 40 varieties of chocolate including haggis chocolate, chilli chocolate and Japanese chocolate, then make your own bar to take home. A TripAdvisor Best of the Best winner and all over TikTok for good reason.

Tip: Book in advance — it sells out fast. Try the whisky truffle making experience for the most Scottish version! Adults £28.
🥐

Twelve Triangles

Artisan Bakery

90 Brunswick Street, Edinburgh EH7 5HU

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's finest artisan bakery — every loaf and pastry is made by hand using slow-fermented cold-prove doughs that take three to four days. The grilled cheese toasties, sourdough, and flaky croissants are extraordinary. Perfect for a quick and delicious mid-morning snack.

Tip: Don't miss the seasonal jam doughnuts — they sell out by midday.

Take a Little Scotland Home

Shopping

From designer boutiques to quirky independents — Edinburgh is a shoppers' delight

🌈

Victoria Street

Most Photogenic

Victoria Street, Edinburgh EH1

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's most beautiful shopping street — a winding rainbow of colourful buildings said to have inspired J.K. Rowling's Diagon Alley. Lined with independent boutiques, artisan cheese shops, whisky stores, and galleries. You're already visiting for the bag making workshop — but leave time to browse.

Tip: Don't miss the Red Door Gallery for Scottish art prints, and Museum Context for Harry Potter collectibles.
🏛️

Multrees Walk

Designer Shopping

Multrees Walk, St Andrew Square, Edinburgh EH1

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's luxury shopping destination — home to Scotland's only Harvey Nichols alongside Louis Vuitton, Mulberry, Burberry, Michael Kors and more. Steps from The Balmoral, this is the place for a designer treat. The Harvey Nichols is exceptional — four floors of the best fashion and beauty in Scotland.

Tip: Harvey Nichols has an excellent beauty hall — perfect for picking up a luxury Scottish skincare brand like Arran Aromatics.
🎩

Grassmarket

Independent Boutiques

Grassmarket, Edinburgh EH1 2JU

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's most characterful shopping area — cobbled streets beneath the castle packed with independent boutiques, vintage shops, and artisan makers. Highlights include Fabhatrix for handmade hats, Armstrong's Vintage for incredible second-hand finds, and Walker & Slater for beautiful Scottish tweed.

Tip: Saturday morning brings a weekly market with local crafts, artisan food, and live music.
📚

Topping & Company Booksellers

Edinburgh's Best Bookshop

2 Blenheim Place, Edinburgh EH7 5JH

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's most magical bookshop — a breathtaking three-floor independent in a beautiful Grade A listed building with floor-to-ceiling shelves and iconic rolling library ladders. Over 70,000 books including signed first editions, and they serve complimentary tea and coffee to every browser. Open 9am–9pm daily. An absolute must.

Tip: Grab a cup of tea, climb a ladder, and lose yourself for an hour. They also do signed author editions and literary events.
🕯️

The Gently Mad

Antiquarian Bookshop

2 Summer Place, Edinburgh EH3 5NR

📍 View on map →

One of Edinburgh's most enchanting hidden gems — a tiny family-run antiquarian bookshop and working bookbindery near the Royal Botanic Garden. Stacked floor to ceiling with rare books, leather-bound classics, vintage illustrated editions and old maps, all watched over by warm lamplight and the scent of aged paper. Pure dark academia magic.

Tip: Combine with a walk through the Royal Botanic Garden — it's a 5-minute stroll. Check hours before visiting as they vary.
👗

George Street

Fashion Hub

George Street, Edinburgh EH2

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's chic fashion thoroughfare — a broad Georgian boulevard lined with stylish brands including Anthropologie, Reiss, Karen Millen, AllSaints, and Oliver Bonas alongside beautiful bars and restaurants. Perfect for a leisurely browse followed by a cocktail at one of the street's gorgeous bars.

Tip: Rox Jewellers on George Street has some stunning Scottish-inspired fine jewellery — a beautiful souvenir.

Worth Popping Into

Quick Sights

Edinburgh is a city of extraordinary beauty around every corner — don't miss these during free time

🏔️

Calton Hill

Best City Views

Calton Hill, Edinburgh EH7 5AA

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's most spectacular viewpoint — a short climb from the city centre rewarded with panoramic 360-degree views of the castle, Arthur's Seat, the Firth of Forth, and the entire city skyline. Home to the unfinished National Monument (Edinburgh's "Disgrace") and the Nelson Monument. A must for golden hour.

Tip: Takes about 15 minutes to walk from The Balmoral. Go just before sunset for the most magical views.
⚰️

Greyfriars Kirkyard

Harry Potter History

Greyfriars Place, Edinburgh EH1 2QQ

📍 View on map →

Edinburgh's most atmospheric graveyard — and the real-life inspiration for Harry Potter. Find Tom Riddle's grave, the graves that inspired other characters, and see the view of George Heriot's School (the real Hogwarts) over the wall. Also home to the statue of Greyfriars Bobby, Edinburgh's most loyal dog.

Tip: Free to enter and takes 20 minutes. The adjacent Greyfriars Bobby pub is a lovely spot for a pint afterwards.
🌊

Dean Village

Hidden Gem

Dean Village, Edinburgh EH4

Edinburgh's most secret neighbourhood — a tucked-away medieval mill village just 10 minutes' walk from Princes Street that feels entirely removed from the busy city. Picturesque stone buildings, a rushing river, and almost no tourists. One of Edinburgh's most beautiful and underrated spots for a wander and photos.

Tip: Walk down from the West End via Bell's Brae for the most dramatic first impression. Free and takes about 30 minutes to explore.
🍺

The Last Drop

Historic Pub

74–78 Grassmarket, Edinburgh EH1 2JR

📍 View on map →

One of Edinburgh's most atmospheric pubs — and its name is delightfully macabre. Built right next to the Grassmarket gallows, it's named after the last public hanging in Edinburgh in 1864. The condemned would reportedly have their final drink here before their execution across the road. Low ceilings, cosy booths, cask ales, great Scottish pub food, and allegedly a resident ghost. A proper Edinburgh experience.

Tip: Grab a window seat with views up to Edinburgh Castle and order a Scotch pie with your pint. The history alone is worth the visit!
🏛️

National Museum of Scotland

Free Entry

Chambers Street, Edinburgh EH1 1JF

📍 View on map →

One of the best free museums in Europe — a spectacular Victorian building housing thousands of years of Scottish and world history. From Dolly the Sheep to ancient Scottish treasures, Viking artefacts, and the story of Scotland's clans and tartans. Perfect for an hour on a rainy afternoon.

Tip: Head to the top floor rooftop terrace for free views of the Old Town skyline — Edinburgh Castle looks incredible from here.

Day 3 Evening · June 3rd

Glasgow

Scotland's most vibrant city — a brilliant mix of architecture, art, world-class bars, and electric nightlife

🎹

The Social Club

Piano Bar · Live Music

42 Union Street, Glasgow G1 3QX

📍 View on map →

Glasgow's beloved piano bar — an intimate, lively venue where a resident pianist plays everything from pop to jazz while guests sing along, drink cocktails, and dance. This is the kind of place where birthdays become legends. Expect a beautifully dressed crowd, theatrical cocktails, and an atmosphere that builds all night long.

Tip: Request a song for Joyce! The pianists here are brilliant improvisers and love a birthday request.
🦩

Tiki Bar & Kitsch Inn

Glasgow Institution

214 Bath Street, Glasgow G2 4HW

📍 View on map →

One of Glasgow's most iconic and outrageously fun bars — a riot of colour, kitsch décor, and brilliant cocktails served in ceramic skulls and tiki mugs. The upstairs Kitsch Inn has a completely different vibe — retro wallpaper, mismatched furniture, and a gloriously eccentric atmosphere. The kind of place you stay for three hours when you only planned one.

Tip: Order the "Glasgow Punch Bowl" for sharing — it's theatrical, enormous, and perfect for a group of women celebrating.
🍝

Café Zique

Dinner · West End

66 Hyndland Street, Glasgow G11 5PT

📍 View on map →

Glasgow's most charming neighbourhood bistro — a French-inspired café and wine bar tucked into the beautiful West End. Gorgeous seasonal dishes, an exceptional natural wine list, and the kind of warm, unhurried atmosphere that makes an evening stretch beautifully. Perfect for a group dinner before a night out.

Tip: Book well in advance — this tiny gem fills up fast. The charcuterie board and steak frites are essential orders.
🍸

Alston Bar & Beef

Cocktails · City Centre

Grand Central Hotel, 99 Gordon St, Glasgow G1 3SF

📍 View on map →

Nestled in the vaulted Victorian underbelly of the Grand Central Hotel — one of Glasgow's most atmospheric and beautiful drinking spots. Stone arches, moody lighting, exceptional cocktails, and the kind of setting that makes you feel like you're in a different era. The cocktail menu is playful and Scottish-inspired.

Tip: Start here for pre-dinner cocktails — the arched Victorian ceilings make for an incredible backdrop. Ask for the bar manager's seasonal cocktail recommendation.
🏛️

Kelvingrove Art Gallery

Free Entry · Daytime

Argyle Street, Glasgow G3 8AG

📍 View on map →

One of the most beautiful free museums in Scotland — a stunning Spanish Baroque sandstone building housing Dalí's Christ of Saint John of the Cross, a hanging Spitfire, Egyptian mummies, and one of the finest art collections in the UK. Set in Kelvingrove Park, it's equally breathtaking from the outside. Free entry, takes about an hour.

Tip: There's a daily organ recital at 1pm — completely free and absolutely magical in the grand central hall. The park outside is perfect for a picnic in good weather.
🛍️

Princes Square & Buchanan Street

Best Shopping in Scotland

Buchanan Street, Glasgow G1

📍 View on map →

Glasgow genuinely has better shopping than Edinburgh — Buchanan Street is Scotland's finest retail thoroughfare, and Princes Square is a stunning Victorian-era atrium mall filled with boutiques, design stores, and great restaurants. Also worth exploring: Argyll Arcade for jewellery, Merchant City for independent boutiques, and the gorgeous Mackintosh-designed streets of the West End.

Tip: Duck into Princes Square just for the building — the ornate iron balconies and peacock sculptures make it one of Scotland's most beautiful interiors.
🎷

Oran Mór

Bar · Music · West End Icon

731 Great Western Road, Glasgow G12 8QX

📍 View on map →

A converted church at the top of Byres Road that's become one of Glasgow's most iconic venues — a bar, restaurant, nightclub, and arts space all under one glorious painted ceiling. The ceiling mural by Alasdair Gray is spectacular. A brilliant place for a drink in the West End with a truly unique atmosphere. Live music nights are legendary.

Tip: Look up — the entire ceiling is a stunning hand-painted mural. The beer garden out back is one of Glasgow's most popular spots on a warm evening.

Days 3 & 4 · Cameron House

Loch Lomond

Scotland's most romantic loch — castles, villages, lochside walks, and pubs with views that take your breath away

🐄

Luss Village

Most Picturesque Village

Luss, Loch Lomond G83 8NY

📍 View on map →

Scotland's most photographed village — a cluster of impossibly pretty stone cottages draped in roses, right on the shores of Loch Lomond. Wander the cobbled lanes, visit the ancient 13th-century parish church, and take the short walk to the loch beach for views of Ben Lomond across the water. Just 5 minutes from Cameron House.

Tip: Go early morning before the tour buses arrive — the village is magical in the quiet morning light. The Highland Coo statues near the beach are iconic photo spots.
🍺

The Luss Tavern

Lochside Pub

Luss, Loch Lomond G83 8NY

📍 View on map →

The quintessential Scottish pub lunch spot — a stone-built village inn right in Luss with an outdoor terrace looking directly out over Loch Lomond. Traditional Scottish pub food done beautifully: cullen skink, haggis, fresh sandwiches, and local ales. Sitting outside here on a sunny afternoon with Loch Lomond glittering in front of you is one of Scotland's great simple pleasures.

Tip: Grab a table on the terrace and order the cullen skink — it's one of the best versions in Scotland. Perfect for a relaxed group lunch.
🛶

Sweeney's Cruises

Loch Cruise · Balloch

Balloch Road, Balloch G83 8LQ

📍 View on map →

The best way to see Loch Lomond is from the water — Sweeney's runs scenic cruises across the loch, past the wooded islands of Inchmurrin and Inchcailloch and up toward the Highland boundary. A one-hour cruise gives you the full sweep of Ben Lomond, the islands, and Cameron House itself from the water. Utterly gorgeous on a clear day.

Tip: Book the private charter for your group — worth it entirely. Bring a bottle of prosecco and let Ben Lomond be your backdrop.
🏰

Balloch Castle & Country Park

Free · 200 Acres

Balloch Castle Country Park, Balloch G83 8LX

📍 View on map →

A beautiful 200-acre country park on the southern shores of Loch Lomond — woodland trails, walled gardens, and the early 19th-century Balloch Castle overlooking the loch. Free entry and perfect for a morning walk with spectacular views. The lochside path gives gorgeous views of the water and the mountains beyond.

Tip: The walled garden is especially beautiful in early June when everything is in bloom. Combine with Luss for a perfect morning out.
🧀

Loch Lomond Shores

Shopping · Cafés · Waterfront

Ben Lomond Way, Balloch G83 8QL

📍 View on map →

The waterfront retail and leisure hub at Balloch — a cluster of Scottish artisan shops, cafés, and restaurants right at the foot of the loch. Excellent for picking up quality Scottish gifts, local food and drink, cashmere, and whisky. The Loch Lomond Whisky & Visitor Centre is here, along with a good Marks & Spencer Food for loch picnic supplies.

Tip: The M&S Simply Food here is perfect for stocking up on picnic supplies, snacks, or a bottle of something for the hotel room. The lochside walk from here to the pier is lovely.
🌿

Cameron House Lochside Walk

Best Morning Activity

Cameron House, Loch Lomond G83 8QZ

📍 View on map →

The grounds of Cameron House sit right on the banks of Loch Lomond — the lochside walk from the hotel grounds gives views of Ben Lomond, the islands, and the water in the early morning light that are genuinely unforgettable. There's a beautiful fallen tree at the water's edge that's become one of the area's most famous photo spots. Go before breakfast while the mist is still on the water.

Tip: This is the ideal spot for your group photo session with the Scottish dresses — golden hour light on Loch Lomond with Ben Lomond behind you. Photographer Paul Saunders (paulsaundersphotography.com) knows this exact spot well.
🧖‍♀️

Cameron House Spa

On-Site · Book Early

Cameron House, Loch Lomond G83 8QZ

📍 View on map →

The award-winning spa at Cameron House is one of Scotland's finest — a stunning loch-view facility with indoor and outdoor pools, a thermal suite, and a full treatment menu. Treatments fill up fast, especially for groups. Even an hour in the thermal suite with views over Loch Lomond is an extraordinary experience. Perfect for a morning before lunch at Luss.

Tip: Book spa treatments at the same time as your hotel reservation — they sell out weeks in advance, especially for groups wanting the same time slot.
